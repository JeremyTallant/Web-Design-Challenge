Latitude vs. Wind Speed

Wind speeds tend to be weaker near the equator because the earth's rotation causes air to rise near the equator, creating a low-pressure area. This rising air creates an area of low wind speeds near the equator. As you move away from the equator, the earth's rotation has less of an effect on the air, so wind speeds tend to increase. However, other factors such as the presence of mountains and other landforms can also affect local wind speeds, regardless of the distance from the equator.
